Xian Group Tour Packages with Great Student Discounts

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is a dream destination for history buffs, culture enthusiasts, and adventurous travelers alike. With its rich heritage, iconic landmarks like the Terracotta Warriors, and vibrant street food scene, it’s no wonder students from around the world flock here for an unforgettable experience. The best part? Many tour operators now offer Xian group tour packages with incredible student discounts, making it easier than ever to explore this legendary city without breaking the bank.

Why Xian Should Be on Every Student’s Travel List

Affordable Adventures Await

Traveling as a student often means sticking to a tight budget, but Xian delivers incredible value. From discounted entry fees to student-friendly hostels and cheap yet delicious street food, this city is a budget traveler’s paradise. Many attractions, including the Terracotta Army Museum, offer 50% off for students with a valid ID.

A Living History Lesson

Xian was once the eastern terminus of the Silk Road and served as the capital for multiple dynasties. Walking through its ancient streets feels like stepping into a history textbook. Must-visit sites include:

  • The Terracotta Warriors – One of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century.
  • The Ancient City Wall – Rent a bike and cycle along this well-preserved fortification.
  • The Muslim Quarter – A bustling hub of culture, spices, and mouthwatering snacks like roujiamo (Chinese hamburger) and yangrou paomo (lamb stew with bread).

Vibrant Nightlife & Student Hangouts

After a day of exploring, Xian’s nightlife won’t disappoint. Bars and clubs near South Gate (Nanmen) and Defu Lane cater to young travelers with affordable drinks and live music. Many hostels also organize pub crawls, making it easy to meet fellow backpackers.

How to Get the Best Student Discounts

1. Always Carry Your Student ID

Many attractions and tour operators require a valid ISIC (International Student Identity Card) or university ID to apply discounts.

2. Book Early & Travel Off-Peak

Prices spike during Chinese holidays like Golden Week (October) and Spring Festival (January/February). For the best deals, travel in March-April or September-November.

3. Join Group Tours Instead of Going Solo

Group tours often negotiate bulk discounts, meaning you’ll pay less for transportation, guides, and entrance fees compared to solo travel.

4. Look for Flash Sales on Travel Apps

Platforms like Klook, Trip.com, and Youth Travel Agency frequently offer last-minute student deals.

Hidden Gems in Xian That Most Tourists Miss

Banpo Neolithic Village

A lesser-known archaeological site showcasing 6,000-year-old ruins—perfect for anthropology students.

Tangbo Art Museum

A free museum featuring contemporary Chinese art, often overlooked by mainstream tours.

Qinglong Temple

A serene Buddhist temple with fewer crowds than the more famous Giant Wild Goose Pagoda.

Final Tips for Student Travelers

  • Bargain at markets – Vendors in the Muslim Quarter expect haggling, so don’t pay the first price quoted.
  • Use public transport – Xian’s metro is cheap and efficient.
  • Learn basic Mandarin phrases – While many young locals speak English, knowing phrases like "duōshǎo qián?" (How much?) helps.

With its unbeatable mix of history, culture, and affordability, Xian is the perfect destination for student travelers. Whether you’re marveling at ancient warriors or slurping down spicy noodles, these group tour packages with student discounts make it easier than ever to experience it all.
