How to Book a Hotel in Xi’an During Major Festivals

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is a magnet for travelers year-round, but it truly comes alive during major festivals like Chinese New Year, the Mid-Autumn Festival, and the Golden Week holidays. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and iconic landmarks like the Terracotta Warriors and the City Wall, Xi’an is a must-visit destination. However, booking a hotel during peak seasons can be a challenge. Here’s your ultimate guide to securing the best accommodations without the stress.

Why Xi’an During Festivals is Special

Cultural Celebrations

Major festivals in Xi’an are a spectacle. During Chinese New Year, the city lights up with lantern displays, temple fairs, and fireworks. The Mid-Autumn Festival brings mooncake feasts and night markets, while Golden Week (October 1-7) sees parades and special performances. Staying in Xi’an during these times lets you experience traditions that date back centuries.

Crowds and High Demand

With popularity comes competition. Hotels fill up months in advance, and prices skyrocket. If you don’t plan ahead, you might end up overpaying or settling for less-than-ideal lodging.

Step-by-Step Guide to Booking Your Hotel

1. Research Early (6-12 Months Ahead)

Festival periods are the busiest, so start your search as early as possible. Use platforms like Booking.com, Agoda, or Trip.com to compare prices and read reviews.

Pro Tip:

Set price alerts on apps like Kayak or Skyscanner to track fluctuations.

2. Choose the Right Location

Xi’an is vast, and staying in the right area saves time and money.

Top Neighborhoods:

  • City Center (Near Bell Tower): Perfect for first-timers—close to attractions, shopping, and metro lines.
  • Muslim Quarter: Ideal for food lovers; bustling but noisy.
  • Qujiang New District: Upscale and quieter, great for families.

3. Understand Hotel Types

From hostels to luxury resorts, Xi’an has it all.

Budget Options:

  • Hostels like Xi’an Han Tang Inn (clean, social vibe).
  • Budget chains like Hanting Hotel (reliable, no frills).

Mid-Range to Luxury:

  • Sofitel Legend Peoples Grand Hotel Xi’an: Historic charm meets modern luxury.
  • Hyatt Regency Xi’an: Rooftop bar with city views.

4. Book Directly vs. Third-Party Sites

Third-party sites offer deals, but booking directly with the hotel can unlock perks:
- Free upgrades.
- Flexible cancellation policies.
- Loyalty points.

5. Check Cancellation Policies

Festival travel plans can change. Opt for free cancellation when possible.

6. Consider Alternative Accommodations

If hotels are booked, try:
- Airbnb: Local apartments with kitchen access.
- Guesthouses (民宿 minsu): Family-run stays for a cultural touch.

Festival-Specific Tips

Chinese New Year (Late Jan – Early Feb)

  • Book by September—the earlier, the better.
  • Expect closures on New Year’s Eve; stock up on snacks.

Golden Week (Oct 1-7)

  • Avoid arriving on Oct 1—transport chaos.
  • Day trips (e.g., Hua Shan) sell out; book tours early.

Mid-Autumn Festival (Sept/Oct)

  • Hotels near Big Wild Goose Pagoda fill fast for moon-viewing events.

Money-Saving Hacks

  • Bundle Deals: Some sites offer discounts when booking flights + hotels.
  • Last-Minute Apps: Try “Lastminute” or “HotelTonight” for unsold rooms.
  • Local Discounts: Some hotels offer lower rates for WeChat or Alipay users.

Final Checklist Before Booking

  1. Confirm the hotel’s proximity to public transport.
  2. Verify if breakfast is included (a lifesaver during busy mornings).
  3. Check if the staff speaks English if you need assistance.

Xi’an during festivals is unforgettable—but only if you plan smartly. Follow these steps, and you’ll secure a cozy base to explore the city’s magic without the booking nightmares. Happy travels!
