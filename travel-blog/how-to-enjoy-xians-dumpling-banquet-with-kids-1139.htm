How to Enjoy Xian’s Dumpling Banquet with Kids

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is famous for its rich history, the Terracotta Warriors, and—most importantly—its incredible food. Among the must-try culinary experiences is the legendary Dumpling Banquet, a feast featuring dozens of creatively shaped and flavored dumplings. But how do you make this dining adventure enjoyable for kids? Here’s your ultimate guide to turning a dumpling banquet into a fun-filled family experience.

Why Xian’s Dumpling Banquet is Perfect for Families

Unlike a typical restaurant meal, a dumpling banquet is an interactive, visual, and delicious journey. Kids love the playful shapes (think duck, fish, or even tiny hedgehog dumplings), while parents appreciate the cultural immersion. Plus, the variety ensures even picky eaters find something they like.

Top Dumpling Banquet Spots for Families

Not all dumpling restaurants are created equal. Here are the most kid-friendly spots in Xian:

  1. Defachang Dumpling Restaurant – A historic venue with a lively atmosphere and colorful dumpling displays.
  2. Jia San Soup Dumplings – Smaller, more casual, and perfect for families who want a quicker meal.
  3. De Fa Chang (钟楼店) – Located near the Bell Tower, this branch offers an English menu and high chairs.

Making the Dumpling Banquet Fun for Kids

Turn It Into a Game

Kids get bored easily, so why not make the meal a game? Here’s how:

  • "Guess the Filling" Challenge – Have kids take a bite and guess the ingredients.
  • Dumpling Art Contest – Bring crayons and let them draw their favorite dumpling shapes.
  • Counting Game – See who can eat the most varieties (bonus points for trying something new!).

Order Wisely

Some dumplings are more kid-friendly than others. Stick to these safe bets:

  • Steamed Dumplings (Jiaozi) – Simple, soft, and usually filled with pork or veggies.
  • Sweet Dumplings (Tangyuan) – Kids love the sticky, sugary ones with sesame or red bean paste.
  • Mini Soup Dumplings (Xiaolongbao) – Fun to poke and sip the broth (just warn them it’s hot!).

Avoid overly spicy or exotic fillings (like lamb with cumin) unless your child is adventurous.

Pairing the Banquet with Kid-Friendly Activities

A dumpling feast is just one part of the Xian experience. Keep the excitement going with these nearby attractions:

Visit the Xian City Wall

Rent a family bike and ride along the ancient wall—kids will love the views and the sense of adventure.

Explore the Muslim Quarter

After dinner, stroll through the bustling Muslim Quarter. Let the kids try persimmon cakes or sweet rose cakes from street vendors.

Watch the Tang Dynasty Show

Some dumpling banquets include a cultural performance with music and dance. The colorful costumes and lively acts will keep kids entertained.

Practical Tips for a Smooth Experience

  • Go Early – Dumpling banquets are popular, so avoid long waits by dining at 5:30 PM.
  • Bring Wet Wipes – Dumplings can get messy, especially with little hands.
  • Ask for Kid-Friendly Chopsticks – Some restaurants offer training chopsticks or forks.
  • Skip the Tea – Opt for fresh juice or yogurt drinks instead.

With the right approach, Xian’s dumpling banquet can be a highlight of your family trip—delicious, educational, and unforgettable!
