Xi’an’s Summer Kite Flying Spots & Festivals

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The ancient city of Xi’an, a place where history whispers from every terracotta soldier and ancient city wall, transforms into a vibrant, dynamic playground each summer. While the archaeological wonders rightfully command global attention, a lighter, more colorful tradition takes to the skies, offering a breathtaking contrast to the earthly treasures below. Kite flying is not merely a pastime here; it is a cultural ritual, a dance between wind and human ingenuity that paints the heavens with swirling dragons, soaring eagles, and intricate geometric patterns. For the traveler seeking an experience beyond the museum, exploring Xi’an’s summer kite culture is an unmissable journey into the heart of its living traditions.

The art of kite flying, or fang zhen (放飞), is deeply woven into the fabric of Chinese history, with origins dating back over two millennia. It’s said that kites were first invented in China for military purposes—measuring distances, testing the wind, and even sending signals. Over centuries, they evolved into tools of celebration, artistic expression, and spiritual symbolism. In Xi’an, the former capital of the Tang Dynasty, this tradition found a fertile home. Today, flying a kite is believed to bring good luck and health, as one literally sends their troubles and illnesses up into the wind to be carried away. A summer’s day in Xi’an is incomplete without looking up to see the sky alive with these fluttering canvases.

Prime Spots for Skyward Soaring in Xi’an

Finding the perfect launchpad is key to a successful kite-flying adventure. Xi’an offers a variety of locations, each with its own unique ambiance and advantages, catering to both novice flyers and seasoned pros.

1. The City Wall (Xi’an Chengqiang)

There is arguably no more iconic or spectacular place to fly a kite in all of China than atop the magnificent Xi’an City Wall. This 14th-century Ming Dynasty fortification, one of the largest and best-preserved in the world, provides an unobstructed 360-degree arena 12 meters above the bustling city. The wide, flat walkway offers plenty of space for a running start, and the constant breezes that sweep across the top are perfect for lifting even the most ambitious kites. The experience is surreal: you’re engaging in an ancient hobby while walking on an ancient structure, with a panoramic view of old and new Xi’an unfolding beneath you. It’s a photographer’s dream and an absolute must-do. Be aware that there is an entrance fee to the wall, and it can get crowded on weekends, so an early morning or weekday evening visit is ideal.

2. Giant Wild Goose Pagoda (Da Cien Temple Complex)

The expansive public squares and parks surrounding the majestic Giant Wild Goose Pagoda are a hub of local life and a favorite gathering spot for kite enthusiasts. The large, open spaces, particularly the North Square with its famous musical fountains, provide ample room for families and groups to fly their kites without congestion. The backdrop is simply stunning—your colorful kite dancing against the serene silhouette of the 1,300-year-old Buddhist pagoda creates a perfect fusion of past and present. The atmosphere here is lively and festive, with street vendors selling everything from simple, affordable kites for children to more elaborate handmade designs. It’s a fantastic place to feel the pulse of the city’s contemporary culture while participating in a timeless tradition.

3. Qujiang Pool Site Park

For those seeking a more serene and picturesque setting, Qujiang Pool Site Park is an excellent choice. This vast park, built on the ruins of the royal garden of the Tang Dynasty, is adorned with lush greenery, tranquil lakes, and winding paths. The open lawns near the water are particularly ideal for kite flying. The breeze coming off the lake is steady and gentle, perfect for a relaxed afternoon of flying. The environment is more peaceful and family-oriented than the bustling squares, making it perfect for a picnic where kids can run free with their kites while parents relax on the grass. It offers a beautiful, natural counterpoint to the urban kite-flying experience on the City Wall.

4. Fenghe River Sluice

A favorite among the city’s dedicated kite-flying community, this spot is located a bit further out from the city center but is well worth the trip for serious enthusiasts. The area near the sluice gate on the Fenghe River offers vast, open fields with incredibly consistent and strong wind conditions, making it the preferred location for flying large, complex, or professional-grade kites that require more power and space. On any given summer weekend, you’ll witness a spectacular show of massive centipedes, intricate boxes, and powerful parafoils darting and diving high above the river. It’s less of a tourist spot and more of a local hangout, offering an authentic glimpse into the passionate subculture of Xi’an’s kite flyers.

The Crown Jewel: Xi’an International Kite Festival

While summer flying is a daily delight, the absolute pinnacle of the kite calendar is the Xi’an International Kite Festival. Typically held in April or May, this event bridges the spring and summer, setting the stage for the season of soaring. It is a dazzling spectacle that draws master kite makers and champion flyers from across China and the globe.

The festival is usually held in a vast, open area like the fields near the Fenghe River or a large public park, transformed into a kaleidoscope of color and motion. The air is filled with a breathtaking array of kites. Traditional Chinese designs like the Beijing swallow, long dragons, and phoenixes compete for airspace with modern creative kites shaped like cartoon characters, airplanes, and abstract art. Teams from Thailand, Japan, the USA, and Europe bring their unique styles and technical prowess, creating a truly international cultural exchange.

Highlights of the Festival

The festival is more than just flying; it’s a full-blown cultural event. Visitors can wander through stalls showcasing exquisite, hand-painted kites from Weifang (China’s official "Kite Capital"), watch live demonstrations by master craftsmen who construct bamboo-framed kites with painstaking precision, and even participate in workshops to build and paint their own simple kite.

The most thrilling aspect is the flight demonstrations and competitions. Teams perform synchronized "ballet" routines set to music, their kites moving in perfect, graceful unison. Night flying is a particular marvel, as kites equipped with LED lights create a dazzling, star-like display against the dark sky. The energy is infectious, celebratory, and deeply impressive, highlighting the kite not just as a toy, but as a serious art form and sport.

Beyond the String: Kite Shopping and Cultural Souvenirs

No kite-flying experience in Xi’an is complete without acquiring your own. The city is full of places to buy a kite, ranging from cheap, cheerful plastic models to priceless works of art.

Where to Buy Your Kite

The best and most atmospheric place to shop is the Muslim Quarter. Amidst the tantalizing aromas of grilled mutton and roujiamo, you’ll find small shops and street vendors overflowing with kites of all shapes and sizes. This is the place to find traditional designs and practice your bargaining skills. For a more curated selection, especially around the time of the festival, official vendor areas at the event itself offer high-quality, often hand-crafted options. For the ultimate souvenir, seek out specialized kite shops, where artisans sell beautiful, delicate kites made from silk and fine paper, mounted in frames for display. These are true pieces of art that represent the pinnacle of the craft.

A Traveler’s Practical Guide to Kite Flying in Xi’an

Best Time to Go: The ideal kite-flying season runs from late April through September. Summer evenings, from 4 PM until dusk, are particularly pleasant as the heat of the day begins to subside and the light is beautiful for photography.

What to Bring: Sun protection is non-negotiable—hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen. The sun at that altitude on the City Wall is intense. Bring water, comfortable shoes for running and walking, and of course, a camera.

Getting There: The City Wall, Giant Wild Goose Pagoda, and Muslim Quarter are all easily accessible via Xi’an’s efficient metro system. For spots like the Fenghe River, a taxi or ride-sharing app like Didi is your best bet.

Etiquette: Be mindful of your surroundings. Give other flyers plenty of space to avoid tangled lines. Never fly near airports, power lines, or over large crowds where a loss of control could cause injury. Most importantly, embrace the fun and look up—you’re participating in a history that is both ancient and wonderfully alive.
