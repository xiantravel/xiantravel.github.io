Top 5 Things to Do Near Xian Bell Tower

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Standing at the very heart of Xi'an for over six centuries, the Bell Tower is more than just a magnificent example of Ming Dynasty architecture; it's the pulsating epicenter of the city. Its commanding position within the massive roundabout means every adventure in Xi'an begins from this point. While the climb to the top offers a classic panoramic view, the true magic lies in stepping out from its shadow and into the labyrinth of life that surrounds it. This isn't just an area of historical monuments; it's a living, breathing, and delicious tapestry of ancient culture and modern energy. Forget a simple checklist—here are the top five immersive experiences that will define your visit to the world around the Xi'an Bell Tower.

1. Get Deliciously Lost in the Muslim Quarter

Directly northwest of the Bell Tower, accessed through a series of bustling lanes, lies not just a "quarter" but a full-sensory immersion into the Hui culture. This is where your itinerary should dissolve in favor of pure, unguided exploration.

A Culinary Pilgrimage for the Fearless Foodie

This is the undisputed king of street food in Xi'an. Come with an empty stomach and a curious palate. The narrow, cobbled street of Beiyuanmen is a symphony of sizzling woks, steaming baskets, and the enthusiastic calls of vendors. Your mission is to graze. Start with the iconic yangrou paomo, a hearty stew of shredded flatbread and lamb you break apart with your fingers—a ritual as satisfying as the meal itself. Follow the aromatic trails to sizzling skewers of chuan'r (cumin-spiced lamb kebabs), grab a paper-thin roujiamo (often called the Chinese hamburger), and don't miss the sweet, sticky delight of persimmon cakes fried to order. It’s a living museum of flavors where every few steps presents a new, irresistible exhibit.

More Than Just Food: Souvenirs and Serenity

Beyond the edible wonders, the maze of side alleys is a treasure trove for unique souvenirs. Look for intricate paper-cuts, beautiful blue Jingdezhen porcelain, shadow puppets, and replicas of the Terracotta Warriors. As the crowds thicken, seek a moment of peace at the Great Mosque of Xi'an, one of the oldest and most serene in China. Tucked away behind a non-descript wall, its tranquil courtyards and pagoda-style architecture blend Islamic and Chinese traditions in a way that is utterly unique and profoundly calming—a world away from the bustling street just outside its gates.

2. Walk the Ancient City Walls from the South Gate (Yongningmen)

A mere 15-minute walk south from the Bell Tower brings you to the most imposing and well-preserved ancient city wall in all of China. Enter through the magnificent South Gate (Yongningmen), and you'll step onto a 14th-century engineering marvel that offers a completely different perspective on Xi'an.

Cycling the Battlements: The Best View in Town

Walking a section is impressive, but to truly grasp the scale, rent a bicycle from one of the rental stations on top of the wall. A full circuit is about 8.5 miles (13.7 kilometers), but even a shorter ride from the South Gate to the West Gate and back is unforgettable. Glide along the wide, stone-paved path, with the ancient crenellations on one side and a stunning view of the city's evolving skyline on the other—from historic rooftops peeking out to modern towers in the distance. It’s a peaceful, elevated journey that physically connects you to the city's defensive history.

Golden Hour and Nightfall Magic

Time your visit for late afternoon. As the sun begins to set, the warm light bathes the grey bricks in gold, creating a photographer's paradise. Stay as night falls to witness the wall and its watchtowers illuminated, with the Bell Tower glowing like a central jewel in the city's grid below. The contrast between the serene, ancient wall and the vibrant, modern city lights is a sight you won't forget.

3. Discover the "Three Must-Sees" of Shuyuanmen Cultural Street

East of the Bell Tower, a quieter but equally captivating historical lane awaits. Shuyuanmen, or the "Academy Gate" Street, is a beautifully restored street lined with Qing Dynasty-style architecture. It’s a hub for traditional Chinese arts and crafts, often referred to as the street of the "Three Must-Sees."

Ink, Stones, and Replicas

First, this is the best place in Xi'an to purchase high-quality calligraphy supplies. Browse shops overflowing with rice paper, ink stones, brushes, and seals. Second, it's a paradise for collectors of replica stone steles and rubbings. Artists here meticulously create rubbings from famous stone inscriptions, allowing you to take home a piece of ancient text. Third, you'll find some of the finest replica Terracotta Warriors and other historical artifacts, often of better craftsmanship than the typical tourist trinkets. The atmosphere is scholarly and relaxed, perfect for a contemplative stroll and meaningful souvenir hunting.

4. Experience the Spectacle of the Bell & Drum Tower Square at Night

You’ve seen the Bell Tower by day. Now, return after dark. The vast public square that encircles the Bell Tower and connects it to the Drum Tower to the north transforms into an epic stage for urban life and light.

A Symphony of Light and Sound

As daylight fades, sophisticated lighting systems bring the twin towers to life. The Bell Tower, bathed in golden light, seems to float above the traffic, while the Drum Tower stands proudly illuminated in red. The fountains in the square often put on choreographed shows, and the entire area buzzes with energy. Locals gather for evening dances, families stroll, and photographers jostle for the perfect shot. It’s a prime spot for people-watching and feeling the dynamic pulse of modern Xi'an against its ancient backdrop. The Drum Tower also holds evening drum performances, a thunderous echo of ancient timekeeping that resonates through the night air.

5. Indulge in a Modern Food & Retail Journey at Century Ginwa

After days of ancient history and street food, a dose of contemporary Chinese luxury provides a fascinating contrast. Just a few blocks west of the Bell Tower sits Century Ginwa, a high-end shopping mall that represents the other side of Xi'an's identity.

Gourmet Exploration and People-Watching

This isn't just about shopping for international brands. Head to its expansive food court and basement gourmet markets. Here, you can sample regional Chinese delicacies in a clean, air-conditioned environment, or find exquisite pastries, imported foods, and beautifully presented local specialties. The upper floors house a range of restaurants from hot pot to fine dining. Sit by a window with a coffee and watch the seamless blend of fashion-forward youth and traditional families that make up today's Xi'an. It’s a reminder that this ancient capital is also a thriving, modern metropolis.

The area around the Bell Tower is a microcosm of Xi'an itself—layered, dynamic, and endlessly fascinating. It invites you to move seamlessly between the 14th and 21st centuries, between solemn history and joyous daily life, between the scent of ancient incense and the aroma of sizzling cumin. Your journey starts at the Bell Tower, but your most cherished memories will be made in the vibrant world that unfolds in every direction from its foundational stones.
