Xi’an’s Best Cultural Travel Blogs to Follow

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is a treasure trove of history, culture, and culinary delights. From the iconic Terracotta Warriors to the vibrant Muslim Quarter, this city offers endless exploration opportunities. For travelers seeking insider tips, hidden gems, and cultural insights, following the right blogs can make all the difference. Here’s a curated list of Xi’an’s best cultural travel blogs to inspire your next adventure.

Top Travel Blogs for Xi’an Explorers

1. The Silk Road Chronicles

Focused on Xi’an’s role as the eastern terminus of the Silk Road, this blog dives deep into the city’s multicultural heritage. Expect detailed guides on historical sites like the Big Wild Goose Pagoda and the Shaanxi History Museum, along with lesser-known stops along the ancient trade route.

Why Follow?
- Expert interviews with historians and local guides
- Stunning photography of Xi’an’s architectural wonders
- Seasonal event highlights, such as the Tang Dynasty Festival

2. Terracotta Traveler

As the name suggests, this blog is a must for anyone fascinated by the Terracotta Army. But it goes beyond the warriors, covering Qin Dynasty history, excavation updates, and even DIY pottery workshops in Xi’an.

Must-Read Posts:
- "Behind the Scenes: Restoring the Terracotta Warriors"
- "How to Avoid Crowds at the Terracotta Army Museum"
- "Hidden Tombs Near Xi’an You’ve Never Heard Of"

3. Xi’an Eats & Streets

Food is a huge part of Xi’an’s culture, and this blog is your ultimate guide to the city’s culinary scene. From yangrou paomo (lamb stew with bread) to biangbiang noodles, the writers explore street food stalls, high-end restaurants, and everything in between.

Pro Tips:
- Best time to visit the Muslim Quarter for fresh snacks
- How to order like a local at hole-in-the-wall eateries
- Food tours led by Xi’an chefs

Niche Blogs for Deeper Dives

1. Calligraphy & Hanfu in Xi’an

For those interested in traditional Chinese arts, this blog showcases Xi’an’s calligraphy workshops, Hanfu (traditional clothing) rental services, and cultural festivals. It’s perfect for travelers who want to immerse themselves in Tang Dynasty aesthetics.

Highlights:
- Where to try calligraphy classes near the City Wall
- Hanfu photoshoot locations with historical backdrops
- Annual Tang Dynasty costume parades

2. Xi’an by Bike

Xi’an’s flat terrain makes it ideal for cycling. This blog offers route suggestions, bike rental tips, and safety advice for exploring the city on two wheels.

Popular Routes:
- Cycling the Ming Dynasty City Wall at sunset
- Scenic ride to Huaqing Palace hot springs
- Countryside adventures to nearby villages

3. Temple Hopper Xi’an

Buddhist and Taoist temples are scattered throughout Xi’an, each with unique stories. This blog covers serene spots like the Daxingshan Temple and the Eight Immortals Temple, along with meditation retreats.

Insider Knowledge:
- Temple etiquette for foreign visitors
- Best times for quiet reflection (hint: early mornings)
- Volunteer opportunities at local monasteries

Up-and-Coming Blogs to Watch

1. Xi’an Underground

This edgy blog focuses on the city’s modern subcultures, from indie music venues to contemporary art galleries. It’s a great resource for travelers tired of the usual tourist trails.

Featured Spots:
- Livehouse bars with local bands
- Street art hotspots in the arts district
- Pop-up markets selling handmade crafts

2. Family Travel Xi’an

Traveling with kids? This blog offers family-friendly itineraries, kid-approved food picks, and interactive museums like the Xi’an Children’s Science Park.

Parent-Approved Activities:
- Terracotta Warriors scavenger hunts
- Dumpling-making classes for little chefs
- Parks with playgrounds near major attractions

3. Xi’an for Digital Nomads

With coworking spaces and café culture on the rise, this blog caters to remote workers exploring Xi’an long-term.

Nomad Essentials:
- Best cafés with strong Wi-Fi
- Affordable monthly stays in quieter neighborhoods
- Networking events for expats and travelers

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or off-the-beaten-path adventurer, these blogs offer fresh perspectives on Xi’an. Bookmark a few before your trip to unlock the city’s deepest secrets!
