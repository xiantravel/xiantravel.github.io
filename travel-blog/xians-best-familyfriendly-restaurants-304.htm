Xian’s Best Family-Friendly Restaurants

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is a treasure trove of history, culture, and—most importantly—delicious food. While the city is famous for its Terracotta Warriors and vibrant Muslim Quarter, it’s also a fantastic destination for families. Traveling with kids means finding restaurants that cater to little taste buds while still offering an authentic culinary experience. Whether you’re craving traditional Shaanxi cuisine or international flavors, Xian has plenty of family-friendly dining spots that will keep everyone happy.

Top Picks for Family Dining in Xian

1. Defachang Dumpling Restaurant (德发长饺子馆)

A must-visit for families, Defachang is legendary for its dumplings. Located near the Bell Tower, this restaurant offers an interactive dining experience where kids can watch chefs craft dumplings into adorable shapes like fish, rabbits, and even tiny pandas. The menu features over 100 varieties, from savory pork to sweet lotus paste, ensuring there’s something for every palate.

Why families love it:
- Fun, visually appealing food
- Central location near major attractions
- Private dining rooms for larger groups

2. Jiasan Guan Tang Bao (贾三灌汤包)

No trip to Xian is complete without trying guan tang bao (soup dumplings), and Jiasan is the best place to do it. These delicate steamed buns burst with hot, flavorful broth—just be sure to teach the kids how to eat them without making a mess! The restaurant’s lively atmosphere and quick service make it ideal for families on the go.

Pro tip: Order the lamb and coriander dumplings for a true Shaanxi flavor.

3. Xi’an Biang Biang Noodle House

If your kids love noodles, they’ll be mesmerized by biang biang mian, Xian’s famous wide, hand-pulled noodles. The dramatic process of chefs stretching the dough is entertainment in itself. Topped with chili oil, minced pork, and veggies, these noodles are hearty and satisfying.

Best for:
- Picky eaters (noodles are a universal kid favorite)
- Quick, filling meals after a day of sightseeing

International Flavors for Little Travelers

1. Papa John’s Pizza (钟楼店)

Sometimes, kids (and parents) just need a break from local cuisine. Papa John’s near the Bell Tower delivers familiar flavors with a Xian twist—think lamb and cumin pizza. The casual setting and kid-friendly menu make it a reliable choice.

2. Element Fresh

For healthier options, Element Fresh offers fresh salads, sandwiches, and smoothies. Their kids’ menu includes favorites like pasta and mini burgers, and the bright, airy space is perfect for a relaxed meal.

Sweet Treats & Snacks

1. Sweet Honey Cake (蜂蜜凉糕) at Muslim Quarter

The Muslim Quarter isn’t just for savory snacks—kids will adore feng mi liang gao, a chilled sticky rice cake drizzled with honey and rose syrup. It’s a refreshing treat on a hot day.

2. Ice Peak Orange Soda (冰峰)

This iconic Xian soda is a hit with kids. Pair it with a rou jia mo (Chinese burger) for the ultimate local fast-food experience.

Tips for Dining with Kids in Xian

  • Go early: Popular spots fill up fast, especially during peak travel seasons.
  • Learn a few phrases: Simple Mandarin like “bù là” (not spicy) can help when ordering.
  • Try street food safely: Stick to busy stalls with high turnover for the freshest bites.

From dumpling feasts to noodle-slurping fun, Xian’s family-friendly restaurants make every meal an adventure. Whether you’re exploring ancient sites or modern eateries, these spots ensure that even the youngest travelers leave with full bellies and big smiles.
