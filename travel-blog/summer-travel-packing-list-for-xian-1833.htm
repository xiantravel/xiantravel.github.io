Summer Travel Packing List for Xi’an

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is a mesmerizing blend of history, culture, and modern vibrancy. From the iconic Terracotta Warriors to the bustling Muslim Quarter, this city offers endless adventures. But to make the most of your summer trip, packing smart is key. Here’s your ultimate packing list to ensure comfort, convenience, and style while exploring Xi’an’s treasures.

Clothing: Stay Cool and Comfortable

Summer in Xi’an can be scorching, with temperatures often soaring above 90°F (32°C). Lightweight, breathable fabrics are a must.

Top Picks for Outfits

  • Moisture-wicking T-shirts and tank tops: Opt for quick-dry materials to combat sweat.
  • Linen or cotton pants/skirts: Loose-fitting bottoms keep you cool while visiting temples or historical sites.
  • A wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses: Shield yourself from the intense sun, especially at open-air attractions like the City Wall.
  • Comfortable walking shoes: You’ll log miles exploring the Terracotta Army or hiking Mount Hua. Sneakers or sturdy sandals are ideal.
  • A lightweight scarf or shawl: Useful for covering shoulders when entering religious sites or as a shield against sudden rain.

Evening Wear

Xi’an’s nightlife is lively, especially around the Bell Tower and South Gate. Pack:
- A casual dress or polo shirt for dinners.
- A light jacket or cardigan—summer nights can be breezy.

Gadgets and Accessories: Capture the Adventure

Must-Have Electronics

  • Power bank: Long days of sightseeing drain phone batteries fast.
  • Universal adapter: China uses Type A, C, and I outlets.
  • Compact camera or smartphone with a good lens: The Terracotta Warriors and Giant Wild Goose Pagoda are photo goldmines.
  • Earbuds: Great for long bus rides or flights.

Handy Extras

  • Portable fan: A lifesaver in crowded spots like the Muslim Quarter.
  • Reusable water bottle with a filter: Tap water isn’t drinkable, but filtered bottles save money and reduce plastic waste.

Health and Hygiene: Stay Fresh and Safe

Essentials for Summer Heat

  • Sunscreen (SPF 50+): Reapply often—Xi’an’s UV index is high.
  • Aloe vera gel: Soothe sunburns after a day outdoors.
  • Insect repellent: Mosquitoes can be pesky near parks and rivers.

Personal Care

  • Travel-sized toiletries: Many hotels provide basics, but bring your favorite brands.
  • Hand sanitizer and wet wipes: Street food stalls don’t always have sinks.
  • Prescription medications: Pharmacies may not carry your specific meds.

Travel Documents and Money

Important Papers

  • Passport and visa: Ensure your visa is valid for entry.
  • Printed hotel reservations and itineraries: Helpful if your phone dies.
  • Copies of emergency contacts: Keep one in your bag and one at your hotel.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Cash in small denominations: Many street vendors and taxis don’t accept cards.
  • Alipay or WeChat Pay: Mobile payments are ubiquitous in Xi’an. Set these up before arriving.

Snacks and Hydration: Fuel Your Explorations

Xi’an’s food scene is legendary, but having snacks on hand is wise for long tours.

On-the-Go Snacks

  • Energy bars or nuts: Perfect for hikes at Mount Hua.
  • Dried fruit: A sweet, healthy pick-me-up.

Local Delicacies to Try

  • Roujiamo (Chinese burger): A must-eat street food.
  • Biang Biang noodles: Thick, chewy, and flavorful.

Miscellaneous Must-Haves

  • Foldable tote bag: For souvenirs like replica Terracotta Warriors or calligraphy art.
  • Phrasebook or translation app: English isn’t widely spoken outside tourist areas.
  • Small umbrella: Sudden summer showers are common.

With this packing list, you’re ready to dive into Xi’an’s rich history, savor its culinary delights, and create unforgettable summer memories. Happy travels!
