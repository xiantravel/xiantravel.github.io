Xi'an Travel Packages: A Perfect Blend of Past & Present

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi'an, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history whispers from every corner while modernity thrums with vibrant energy. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or an adventurer, Xi'an travel packages offer something for everyone. From the legendary Terracotta Warriors to the bustling Muslim Quarter, this city seamlessly blends the past and present into an unforgettable journey.

Why Xi'an Should Be on Your Travel Bucket List

Xi'an isn’t just another Chinese city—it’s a living museum. As the starting point of the Silk Road and home to over 3,000 years of history, it’s a must-visit for anyone fascinated by culture, heritage, and epic tales of emperors and dynasties. But beyond its historical treasures, Xi’an is also a dynamic metropolis with trendy cafes, lively night markets, and cutting-edge attractions.

Top Historical Attractions in Xi'an

The Terracotta Warriors: An Army Frozen in Time

No trip to Xi'an is complete without witnessing the awe-inspiring Terracotta Army. Discovered in 1974 by local farmers, this UNESCO World Heritage Site features thousands of life-sized clay soldiers, each with unique facial expressions. The sheer scale and craftsmanship will leave you speechless.

The Ancient City Wall: A Walk Through Time

Built during the Ming Dynasty, Xi'an’s City Wall is one of the best-preserved in China. Rent a bike and cycle along its 14-kilometer perimeter for panoramic views of the old and new city. At sunset, the golden hues cast a magical glow over the ancient bricks.

The Big Wild Goose Pagoda: A Symbol of Buddhist Heritage

This seven-story pagoda stands as a testament to Xi'an’s spiritual legacy. Built during the Tang Dynasty, it once housed Buddhist scriptures brought from India. Today, the surrounding fountain plaza hosts a mesmerizing music and light show at night.

Modern Xi'an: Where Tradition Meets Innovation

While Xi'an is steeped in history, it’s also a city on the move. Here’s how modern travelers can experience its contemporary side:

The Muslim Quarter: A Food Lover’s Paradise

Step into this bustling neighborhood, where the aroma of sizzling lamb skewers and freshly baked naan bread fills the air. Don’t miss yangrou paomo (crumbled bread in lamb soup) or roujiamo (Chinese hamburger). By day, it’s a cultural hub; by night, it transforms into a lively street food carnival.

Qujiang New District: Xi'an’s Cultural & Entertainment Hub

This area is where Xi'an’s creative energy shines. Visit the Tang Paradise theme park, a grand recreation of Tang Dynasty gardens, or catch a performance at the Shaanxi Grand Opera House. The nearby Datang Everbright City dazzles with neon lights and futuristic architecture.

Xi'an’s Coffee & Craft Beer Scene

Believe it or not, Xi'an has a thriving café culture. Specialty coffee shops like Localand and M+ Coffee offer artisanal brews in stylish settings. For beer enthusiasts, craft breweries such as Xian Brewery serve locally inspired ales—perfect after a day of sightseeing.

Unique Xi'an Travel Packages to Consider

The Silk Road Explorer

This package takes you beyond Xi'an to key Silk Road sites like the Dunhuang Mogao Caves and the Rainbow Mountains in Zhangye. Perfect for travelers who want to trace the footsteps of ancient merchants.

The Culinary Adventure Tour

Foodies rejoice! This itinerary includes cooking classes, market tours, and exclusive dining experiences—from imperial banquets to hole-in-the-wall noodle joints.

The Family-Friendly Heritage Trip

Designed for families, this package balances education and fun. Kids can participate in Terracotta Warrior workshops, kite-flying on the City Wall, and interactive museum exhibits.

Tips for Planning Your Xi'an Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer pleasant weather. Avoid summer crowds if possible.
  • Transportation: The metro is efficient, but taxis and Didi (China’s Uber) are affordable for getting around.
  • Language Tips: While English isn’t widely spoken, learning a few Mandarin phrases (or using translation apps) helps.
  • Cultural Etiquette: Respect local customs, especially in religious sites. Dress modestly when visiting temples.

Xi'an is more than a destination—it’s an experience. Whether you’re marveling at ancient wonders or savoring modern delights, this city promises a journey through time like no other.
