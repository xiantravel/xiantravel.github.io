The Best Cafés & Restaurants Near Xian Bell Tower

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity blend seamlessly. At its heart stands the iconic Bell Tower, a magnificent structure that has witnessed centuries of change. Surrounding this landmark is a vibrant culinary scene, offering everything from traditional Shaanxi flavors to trendy international cafés. Whether you're a foodie, a coffee enthusiast, or a traveler looking for a memorable dining experience, here’s your ultimate guide to the best cafés and restaurants near Xi’an Bell Tower.

Traditional Shaanxi Cuisine

1. Defachang Dumpling Restaurant

A must-visit for anyone craving authentic Shaanxi dumplings, Defachang is legendary. Located just a short walk from the Bell Tower, this restaurant serves over 100 varieties of dumplings, each with unique fillings and shapes. The Guantang Baozi (soup dumplings) are a standout—bite carefully to savor the rich broth inside.

2. Lao Sun Jia Muslim Restaurant

Xi’an’s Muslim Quarter is famous for its street food, but Lao Sun Jia offers a sit-down experience with equally delicious fare. Their Yangrou Paomo (crumbled bread in lamb stew) is a local specialty. Tear the bread into tiny pieces, let it soak in the flavorful broth, and enjoy a dish that’s been loved for generations.

3. Biang Biang Noodles

No trip to Xi’an is complete without trying Biang Biang Mian, named after the sound the dough makes when slapped against the counter. These thick, hand-pulled noodles are served with chili oil, garlic, and your choice of toppings. Look for small, family-run spots near the Bell Tower for the most authentic experience.

Modern Cafés & Brunch Spots

1. Starry Sky Café

A cozy retreat with a rooftop view of the Bell Tower, Starry Sky Café is perfect for a relaxed afternoon. Their pour-over coffee is expertly brewed, and the matcha latte comes highly recommended. Pair your drink with a slice of their homemade cheesecake for the ultimate indulgence.

2. The Morning After

This trendy brunch spot caters to both locals and expats with its Western-style menu. Avocado toast, fluffy pancakes, and eggs Benedict are crowd-pleasers. The industrial-chic décor and friendly vibe make it a great place to unwind after exploring the city.

3. Maan Coffee

A Korean-inspired chain with a location near the Bell Tower, Maan Coffee offers a luxurious setting with plush seating and elegant interiors. Their signature Dalgona Coffee and Honey Bread are Instagram-worthy and delicious. It’s an ideal spot for digital nomads or anyone needing a quiet place to work.

Hidden Gems & Unique Eateries

1. Xi’an Impression

Tucked away in a narrow alley, this small restaurant is a favorite among locals for its Roujiamo (Chinese hamburger). The crispy bread and tender braised pork filling make it a must-try. Arrive early—this place gets packed during lunch hours.

2. The Alley of Spice

For those who love bold flavors, this unassuming eatery serves fiery Sichuan dishes just a stone’s throw from the Bell Tower. Their Mapo Tofu and Hot Pot are legendary, but be prepared for the heat!

3. Tang West Market Brewery

A fusion of history and craft beer, this microbrewery is set in a restored Tang Dynasty-style building. Try their Persimmon Ale, a local twist on a classic brew, while enjoying live music on weekends.

Sweet Treats & Dessert Cafés

1. Sweet Talk Dessert House

This pastel-colored café is a paradise for dessert lovers. Their Mango Sago Pomelo and Molten Lava Cake are divine. The whimsical décor makes it a hit with families and couples alike.

2. Xi’an Bell Tower Ice Cream

A small kiosk near the tower serves artisanal ice cream in flavors like Black Sesame and Lychee Rose. Perfect for a quick treat while sightseeing.

3. Chocolate & Love

As the name suggests, this spot is all about decadent chocolate desserts. Their Chocolate Fondue and Tiramisu are heavenly. Pair with a cup of their rich hot chocolate for the ultimate sugar rush.

Tips for Dining Near the Bell Tower

  • Timing Matters: Popular spots fill up quickly, especially during weekends. Arrive early or make reservations if possible.
  • Cash vs. Mobile Pay: While most places accept mobile payments like Alipay, smaller vendors may prefer cash.
  • Explore Side Streets: Some of the best eateries are hidden in narrow alleys—don’t be afraid to wander off the main roads.

From time-honored Shaanxi dishes to contemporary cafés, the area around Xi’an Bell Tower is a food lover’s dream. Every meal here is a chance to taste history, culture, and innovation—all in one bite.
