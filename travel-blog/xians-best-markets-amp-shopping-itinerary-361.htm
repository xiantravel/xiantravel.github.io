Xian’s Best Markets & Shopping Itinerary

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is not just a treasure trove of history but also a paradise for shoppers. From bustling markets brimming with local crafts to modern malls offering luxury brands, the city has something for every traveler. Whether you’re hunting for souvenirs, sampling street food, or indulging in retail therapy, this itinerary will guide you through Xian’s best shopping spots.

The Must-Visit Markets in Xian

Muslim Quarter (Huimin Jie)

No trip to Xian is complete without exploring the Muslim Quarter. This vibrant alleyway is a sensory overload—colorful stalls, sizzling skewers, and the aroma of spices fill the air. Here’s what you shouldn’t miss:

  • Food Galore: Try roujiamo (Chinese hamburger), yangrou paomo (lamb stew with bread), and persimmon cakes.
  • Handicrafts: Look for intricate paper cuttings, shadow puppets, and embroidered fabrics.
  • Spices & Teas: Stock up on fragrant teas and exotic spices like star anise and Sichuan peppercorns.

Pro tip: Visit in the evening when the lanterns light up, and the atmosphere turns magical.

Shuyuanmen Cultural Street

For a more refined shopping experience, head to Shuyuanmen Cultural Street. Lined with traditional Ming and Qing-style architecture, this street is a haven for art lovers and collectors.

  • Calligraphy Supplies: Brush pens, inkstones, and rice paper make perfect gifts.
  • Antiques & Replicas: Find everything from ancient coins to replica terracotta warriors (just ensure they’re legally exportable).
  • Tea Houses: Take a break at a quaint tea house and sip on locally grown Jasmine or Biluochun.

Banpo International Fabric Market

Fashionistas, rejoice! This market is a hidden gem for textiles and tailor-made clothing.

  • Silk & Brocade: Xian was a key stop on the Silk Road, so high-quality silk is a must-buy.
  • Custom Tailoring: Get a qipao or suit made in 24–48 hours at unbeatable prices.
  • Embroidery: Shaanxi-style embroidery is renowned for its intricate designs.

Modern Shopping Destinations

Century Ginwa Shopping Mall

If you crave luxury, Century Ginwa is Xian’s answer to Rodeo Drive.

  • High-End Brands: Gucci, Louis Vuitton, and Cartier all have flagship stores here.
  • Fine Dining: Treat yourself to a meal at one of the mall’s upscale restaurants.
  • Beauty Services: Book a spa session or a traditional Chinese massage.

Metro Plaza

A favorite among locals, Metro Plaza offers a mix of international and domestic brands at mid-range prices.

  • Electronics: Great deals on gadgets, especially if you’re tech-savvy.
  • Fast Fashion: Uniqlo, Zara, and H&M are all present.
  • Cinema & Entertainment: Catch the latest blockbuster after shopping.

Unique Souvenirs to Bring Home

Terracotta Warrior Replicas

A miniature terracotta warrior is the ultimate Xian souvenir. Look for officially licensed ones near the Terracotta Army Museum.

Shaanxi Shadow Puppets

These delicate leather puppets are a UNESCO-recognized art form. They make for stunning wall decorations.

Biangbiang Noodles (Dried Packets)

Can’t get enough of Xian’s famous noodles? Buy vacuum-sealed packs to recreate the dish at home.

Bargaining Tips for Xian’s Markets

  1. Start Low: Offer 30–50% of the initial price and negotiate from there.
  2. Be Polite: A smile goes a long way. Haggling is expected, but aggression isn’t.
  3. Walk Away: If the price isn’t right, pretend to leave—often, the vendor will call you back with a better deal.

Final Shopping Itinerary

  • Morning: Explore Shuyuanmen Cultural Street for artsy finds.
  • Afternoon: Hit the Muslim Quarter for snacks and souvenirs.
  • Evening: Splurge at Century Ginwa or unwind at Metro Plaza.

Xian’s markets and shopping scenes are as diverse as its history. Whether you’re a bargain hunter or a luxury seeker, this city promises an unforgettable retail adventure.
