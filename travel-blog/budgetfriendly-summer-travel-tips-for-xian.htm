Budget-Friendly Summer Travel Tips for Xi’an

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Summer in Xi’an is a vibrant, bustling season filled with rich history, mouth-watering street food, and unforgettable cultural experiences. While the summer heat can be intense, the city’s charm more than makes up for it. Traveling to Xi’an on a budget doesn’t mean you have to miss out on the highlights. With some smart planning and insider tips, you can explore the ancient capital of China without draining your wallet. This guide will walk you through everything from affordable accommodations and cheap eats to free attractions and money-saving transportation hacks.

Getting to Xi’an Without Breaking the Bank

Xi’an is well-connected by air, train, and bus, making it accessible for budget travelers from all over. The key is to book in advance and be flexible with your travel dates.

Finding Cheap Flights

If you’re flying into Xi’an Xianyang International Airport, keep an eye out for airline sales and discounts. Booking your flight at least two months in advance can save you a significant amount. Use flight comparison websites and set up price alerts to snag the best deals. Consider flying on weekdays, as tickets are often cheaper than on weekends. Another tip is to look for flights with layovers, which can be more budget-friendly than direct routes.

Taking the Train

China’s high-speed rail network is efficient and comfortable. Traveling by train to Xi’an can be a scenic and cost-effective option, especially if you opt for second-class seats. The Xi’an Railway Station and Xi’an North Railway Station are major hubs with connections to many cities. Booking train tickets online in advance through official channels ensures you get the best prices. Overnight trains with sleeper berths can also save you a night’s accommodation cost.

Long-Distance Buses

For the ultra-budget traveler, long-distance buses are an option, though they take longer. They are especially useful if you’re traveling from nearby provinces. Bus tickets are usually cheaper than trains and flights, but comfort levels may vary. Always check reviews and choose reputable bus companies for a safer journey.

Affordable Accommodation Options

Where you stay can make or break your budget. Xi’an offers a range of budget-friendly accommodations that don’t compromise on comfort or location.

Hostels and Guesthouses

Xi’an has a thriving hostel scene, particularly in the city center and near the Bell Tower. Hostels like the Han Tang Inn or the Xi’an Han Tang House Hostel offer dormitory beds for as low as $10 per night. Many hostels provide free Wi-Fi, communal kitchens, and social activities, making them great for meeting fellow travelers. Private rooms in guesthouses are also affordable, typically ranging from $20 to $40 per night.

Budget Hotels

If hostels aren’t your style, look for budget hotel chains like Home Inn or 7 Days Inn. These hotels offer clean, basic rooms with private bathrooms at reasonable prices. Booking through apps like Trip.com or Agoda can often net you discounts. Staying slightly outside the main tourist areas, such as in the Lianhu District, can also save you money while still providing easy access to public transport.

Alternative Stays

For longer stays, consider renting a room or apartment through platforms like Airbnb. This can be cost-effective, especially if you’re traveling with a group. Another option is temple stays or university dormitories, though these are less common and may require advance arrangements.

Eating on a Budget: Xi’an’s Street Food and Local Eateries

One of the best parts of visiting Xi’an is the food, and luckily, you don’t need to spend a lot to eat well. The city is famous for its street food, which is both delicious and affordable.

Must-Try Street Foods

Head to the Muslim Quarter (Huimin Jie) for a culinary adventure. Here, you can sample a variety of snacks for just a few dollars. Don’t miss:

  • Roujiamo: Often called the Chinese hamburger, this savory pastry filled with seasoned meat is a must-try. It costs around $1-2.
  • Biang Biang Noodles: These thick, hand-pulled noodles are served with chili oil and vegetables. A bowl typically costs $2-3.
  • Yangrou Paomo: A hearty lamb soup where you break flatbread into pieces and add it to the broth. A serving is about $3-4.
  • Liangpi: Cold skin noodles mixed with sauce and vegetables, perfect for a hot summer day. It’s usually under $2.

Local Restaurants and Food Courts

For sit-down meals, look for small local restaurants away from tourist spots. Noodle shops and dumpling houses offer filling meals for $3-5. Food courts in shopping malls like the one near the Bell Tower provide a variety of options at fixed low prices. Also, try the university canteens near campuses such as Xi’an Jiaotong University, where meals are cheap and authentic.

Saving on Drinks and Snacks

Avoid buying bottled water repeatedly; carry a reusable bottle and refill it at your accommodation or public places. Street vendors sell fresh fruit like watermelon and peaches for a quick, cheap snack. For coffee lovers, skip the international chains and try local tea houses or convenience store coffee to save money.

Free and Low-Cost Attractions

Xi’an is packed with historical and cultural sites, many of which are free or have minimal entry fees. Planning your itinerary around these can keep your budget intact.

Exploring the City Walls and Parks

The Xi’an City Wall is a iconic landmark, and while walking on it requires a ticket (around $8), you can admire it from below for free. The areas around the wall, especially the South Gate, often have free cultural performances or events in the summer. Nearby parks like the Tang Paradise Outer Park offer beautiful landscapes and relaxing spots without an entrance fee.

Free Museums and Temples

The Shaanxi History Museum is a treasure trove of artifacts and offers free entry, though you need to book tickets online in advance due to high demand. The Big Wild Goose Pagoda area has a large public square with music fountains that put on free shows in the evenings. Temples such as the Daxingshan Temple are free to enter and provide a peaceful retreat from the city hustle.

Budget-Friendly Paid Attractions

For must-see sites like the Terracotta Army, look for discounted tickets online or through travel agencies. Student discounts are often available with a valid ID. Consider buying a combination ticket for multiple attractions if you plan to visit several paid sites. The Bell Tower and Drum Tower have joint tickets that save money compared to buying separately.

Getting Around Xi’an Cheaply

Xi’an’s public transportation system is efficient and affordable, making it easy to get around without spending much.

Using the Metro and Buses

The Xi’an Metro has lines covering major attractions, and a single ride costs as little as $0.50. Buy a rechargeable metro card for convenience and small discounts. Buses are even cheaper, with flat fares around $0.30. Use apps like Baidu Maps or Amap to navigate routes and avoid getting lost.

Biking and Walking

Xi’an is a bike-friendly city with numerous bike-sharing services like Mobike and Hello Bike. Renting a bike for short trips can cost under $1 per hour and is a fun way to explore. Many attractions in the city center, such as the Bell Tower and Muslim Quarter, are within walking distance of each other, so you can save on transport by grouping your visits.

Avoiding Taxis and Ride-Sharing

Taxis and ride-sharing apps like Didi can add up quickly. Use them only when necessary, such as late at night or for trips to outskirts like the Terracotta Army. For airport transfers, the airport shuttle bus is a budget-friendly option at around $5, compared to $20-30 for a taxi.

Smart Shopping and Souvenir Tips

Bringing home memories doesn’t have to be expensive. Xi’an has plenty of affordable shopping options for souvenirs and gifts.

Best Places for Bargains

The Muslim Quarter is not just for food; it’s also great for shopping. You can find inexpensive souvenirs like replica Terracotta Warriors, paper cuttings, and calligraphy sets. Haggle politely for better prices. For more variety, check out the wholesale markets near the Bell Tower or the East Street Market, where prices are lower than in tourist shops.

What to Buy on a Budget

Stick to small, lightweight items to save on luggage space and cost. Popular budget souvenirs include:

  • Shadow Puppets: Traditional handicrafts that are unique to the region, costing $2-5.
  • Local Snacks: Packaged items like persimmon cakes or nuts from the Muslim Quarter make great edible gifts.
  • Postcards and Stamps: Available at post offices or souvenir stalls for a few dollars.

Avoiding Tourist Traps

Steer clear of shops inside major attractions like the Terracotta Army museum, where prices are inflated. Instead, buy from vendors outside or in local markets. Be cautious of "free" tours that end up pressuring you to buy overpriced items.

Summer-Specific Tips for Comfort and Savings

Summer in Xi’an can be hot and humid, so staying comfortable without overspending is key.

Beating the Heat

Plan your outdoor activities for early morning or late afternoon to avoid the peak heat. Carry a hat, sunscreen, and a portable fan. Many museums and shopping malls have free air conditioning—use them as cooling breaks. Public parks with shaded areas are also great for resting.

Staying Hydrated and Healthy

Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration. Buy bottled water in bulk from supermarkets instead of tourist spots to save money. Try local teas like chrysanthemum tea, which is refreshing and cheap. Street food is generally safe, but stick to busy vendors with high turnover to ensure freshness.

Enjoying Summer Festivals and Events

Summer often brings cultural festivals and events, many of which are free. Check local listings for things like the Tang Dynasty cultural shows in the parks or temple fairs. These events offer a glimpse into local traditions without the high cost of paid performances.

Additional Money-Saving Hacks

To stretch your budget further, consider these extra tips:

  • Travel During Off-Peak Times: Late summer (August) might have fewer crowds and slightly lower prices compared to June and July.
  • Use Cashback and Reward Apps: Apps like Alipay or WeChat Pay sometimes offer discounts for tourists on attractions and dining.
  • Learn Basic Mandarin Phrases: Knowing a few words like "duōshao qián?" (how much?) can help in negotiations and show respect, potentially leading to better deals.
  • Group Tours for Discounts: If visiting attractions like the Terracotta Army, joining a group tour can be cheaper than going alone, as group rates often apply.

With these budget-friendly tips, your summer trip to Xi’an can be both memorable and affordable. Embrace the city’s history, indulge in its culinary delights, and explore with confidence, knowing you’re getting the most value for your money.
