Xian’s Best Places to Try Exotic Fruits

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is not only famous for its Terracotta Warriors and rich history but also for its vibrant food scene. While street food like roujiamo and yangrou paomo often steal the spotlight, the city also offers a surprising array of exotic fruits that are worth exploring. Whether you’re a fruit enthusiast or a curious traveler, here’s your guide to the best places in Xian to taste unique and delicious fruits from around the world.

The Exotic Fruit Scene in Xian

Xian’s markets and specialty stores have embraced global flavors, making it easier than ever to find fruits that were once considered rare in this part of China. From Southeast Asian favorites to Latin American gems, the city’s vendors and supermarkets are stocked with colorful, flavorful options.

Why Xian is a Great Place for Fruit Lovers

  1. Diverse Markets – Xian’s wholesale markets and night markets often feature seasonal exotic fruits.
  2. Growing Demand – As international travel increases, so does the appetite for global produce.
  3. Local Adaptations – Some fruits are even grown in nearby regions, blending foreign flavors with local freshness.

Top Spots to Find Exotic Fruits in Xian

1. Xian Farmers’ Market (Weekend Special)

If you’re looking for fresh, organic, and sometimes exotic fruits, the Xian Farmers’ Market is a must-visit. Held on weekends, this market attracts vendors selling everything from dragon fruit to mangosteen.

Highlights:
- Dragon Fruit (Pitaya) – Bright pink and packed with antioxidants.
- Mangosteen – Known as the "queen of fruits," this sweet and tangy treat is a must-try.
- Rambutan – A hairy-looking fruit with a juicy, lychee-like interior.

Best Time to Go: Early morning for the freshest picks.

2. Huimin Street (Muslim Quarter)

While Huimin Street is famous for its snacks, some vendors also sell dried and fresh exotic fruits. Look for stalls offering:

  • Dried Longan & Lychee – Sweet, chewy, and perfect for on-the-go snacking.
  • Fresh Figs – Occasionally available in late summer.
  • Persimmons (Shi Zi) – A local favorite, especially in autumn.

Pro Tip: Bargain politely—some vendors may offer discounts if you buy in bulk.

3. Metro Supermarket (High-End Imported Fruits)

For those who prefer a more curated shopping experience, Metro Supermarket offers a selection of imported fruits.

What to Look For:
- Durian – The "king of fruits" is polarizing but worth trying if you’re adventurous.
- Passionfruit – Tart and tropical, great for juices or desserts.
- Cherimoya – A creamy, custard-like fruit from South America.

Why Go Here? The quality is consistent, and you’ll find fruits that are harder to get elsewhere.

4. Xian Wholesale Fruit Market (South Suburb)

For the ultimate fruit haul, head to the Xian Wholesale Fruit Market. This is where many local vendors source their stock, so prices are lower, and the variety is impressive.

Top Picks:
- Jackfruit – Massive and sweet, often sold in pre-cut portions.
- Guava – Crisp and fragrant, best eaten with a sprinkle of plum powder.
- Starfruit (Carambola) – Mildly sweet with a unique star-shaped slice.

Insider Tip: Go with a local friend who can help navigate and negotiate.

Seasonal Exotic Fruits to Watch For

Xian’s fruit offerings change with the seasons. Here’s what to look for throughout the year:

Spring & Summer

  • Lychee – Juicy and floral, best in early summer.
  • Mangoes – Imported from Southeast Asia, super sweet.
  • Wax Apples (Lian Wu) – Crunchy and refreshing.

Autumn & Winter

  • Pomegranates (Shi Liu) – Xian’s own pomegranates are a local pride.
  • Kiwi Berries – Mini kiwis with edible skin.
  • Persimmons – Soft and honey-like when fully ripe.

How to Enjoy Exotic Fruits Like a Local

Many fruits in Xian are enjoyed in unique ways:

  • With Spices – Try mango or guava with chili salt for a sweet-spicy kick.
  • In Desserts – Look for shaved ice topped with tropical fruits.
  • As Juice – Freshly squeezed sugarcane juice with a splash of lime is a street-side favorite.

Final Thoughts

Exploring Xian’s exotic fruit scene is a delicious way to experience the city’s evolving culinary landscape. From bustling markets to high-end supermarkets, there’s always something new to taste. So next time you’re in Xian, skip the usual snacks and take a fruity adventure instead!
