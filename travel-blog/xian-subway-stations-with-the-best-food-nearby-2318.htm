Xi’an Subway Stations with the Best Food Nearby

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is not only famous for its Terracotta Warriors and rich history but also for its vibrant food scene. The city’s subway system is a convenient way to explore, and many stations are surrounded by hidden culinary gems. Whether you’re craving savory roujiamo (Chinese burgers), steaming bowls of biangbiang noodles, or sweet persimmon cakes, these subway stops will lead you to some of the best eats in town.

Line 1: Zhonglou (Bell Tower) Station

Why It’s a Food Hotspot

Zhonglou Station sits at the heart of Xi’an, right next to the iconic Bell Tower and Drum Tower. This area is a paradise for food lovers, with the Muslim Quarter (Huimin Jie) just a short walk away.

Must-Try Dishes Nearby

  • Roujiamo – Often called the "Chinese hamburger," this crispy bread pocket is stuffed with tender, spiced meat. Look for Lao Sun Jia for one of the best versions.
  • Yangrou Paomo – A lamb stew where you tear bread into tiny pieces before soaking them in a rich broth. Tong Sheng Xiang is a legendary spot.
  • Persimmon Cakes (Shizi Bing) – Sweet, sticky, and slightly crispy, these fried treats are perfect for dessert.

Line 2: Beidajie Station

Why It’s a Food Hotspot

Beidajie Station is close to Xi’an’s bustling commercial area, but it’s also a gateway to some fantastic local eateries.

Must-Try Dishes Nearby

  • Biangbiang Noodles – These thick, hand-pulled noodles are served with chili oil, garlic, and sometimes minced pork. You Po Che Mian is a top pick.
  • Cold Noodles (Liangpi) – A refreshing dish made from wheat or rice flour, often served with vinegar and chili.
  • Jianbing Guozhi – A crispy crepe filled with egg, cilantro, and sauces—great for a quick snack.

Line 3: Xiaozhai Station

Why It’s a Food Hotspot

Xiaozhai is one of Xi’an’s busiest shopping districts, but its underground food courts and nearby alleys hide some incredible bites.

Must-Try Dishes Nearby

  • Malatang – A DIY spicy soup where you pick your own ingredients. Yang Guifei Malatang is a local favorite.
  • Xi’an Burgers (Roujiamo Variations) – Some vendors here offer modern twists, like adding pickled vegetables or extra spices.
  • Sour Plum Drink (Suan Mei Tang) – A sweet-and-sour beverage that pairs perfectly with spicy food.

Line 4: Daminggong West Station

Why It’s a Food Hotspot

This station is near the Daming Palace ruins, but the surrounding neighborhoods are packed with authentic local eateries.

Must-Try Dishes Nearby

  • Huluji Soup – A peppery, herbal broth with shredded chicken, popular in colder months.
  • Xi’an Dumplings (Jiaozi) – Boiled or fried, these dumplings come with various fillings. Try De Fa Chang for a classic version.
  • Fried Meatballs (Zha Wanzi) – Crispy on the outside, juicy inside—best eaten fresh from the fryer.

Line 5: Tiyuchang (Sports Center) Station

Why It’s a Food Hotspot

Though newer, this station is near residential areas where locals eat, meaning fewer tourists and more authentic flavors.

Must-Try Dishes Nearby

  • Spicy Crayfish (Xiaolongxia) – A late-night favorite, often served with beer.
  • Xi’an-Style Barbecue (Kao Rou) – Skewers of lamb, beef, or vegetables grilled over charcoal.
  • Stuffed Pancakes (Xian Bing) – Flaky pastries filled with meat or vegetables.

Pro Tips for Food Hunting in Xi’an’s Subway Stations

  • Go Early or Late – Some stalls only operate during peak meal times.
  • Follow the Locals – If a place has a long line, it’s usually worth the wait.
  • Bring Cash – Smaller vendors might not accept digital payments.

Xi’an’s subway doesn’t just transport people—it leads to unforgettable food adventures. Whether you’re hopping off at Zhonglou for a roujiamo or exploring Xiaozhai’s malatang scene, every stop offers a taste of the city’s rich culinary heritage.
