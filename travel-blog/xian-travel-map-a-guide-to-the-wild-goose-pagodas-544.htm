Xian Travel Map: A Guide to the Wild Goose Pagodas

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is a treasure trove of history, culture, and breathtaking landmarks. Among its many attractions, the Wild Goose Pagodas stand out as iconic symbols of the city’s rich Buddhist heritage. Whether you’re a history buff, a spiritual seeker, or simply a traveler looking for stunning architecture, these pagodas are a must-visit. This guide will take you through everything you need to know about the Wild Goose Pagodas, including their history, how to visit, and nearby hotspots to explore.

The History of the Wild Goose Pagodas

The Great Wild Goose Pagoda (Da Yan Ta)

Built in 652 AD during the Tang Dynasty, the Great Wild Goose Pagoda was originally constructed to house Buddhist scriptures brought from India by the famous monk Xuanzang. Standing at 64 meters tall, this seven-story pagoda is a masterpiece of ancient Chinese architecture. Its brick structure has withstood earthquakes and the test of time, making it a testament to the ingenuity of Tang Dynasty engineers.

The Small Wild Goose Pagoda (Xiao Yan Ta)

Just a short distance away, the Small Wild Goose Pagoda offers a quieter but equally fascinating experience. Built in 707 AD, this 13-story pagoda is slightly smaller but no less impressive. Unlike its larger counterpart, the Small Wild Goose Pagoda has a more delicate design and is surrounded by the serene Jianfu Temple grounds.

How to Visit the Wild Goose Pagodas

Best Time to Go

The pagodas are stunning year-round, but spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer the most pleasant weather. Early mornings or late afternoons are ideal to avoid crowds and capture the best photos.

Ticket Information

  • Great Wild Goose Pagoda: ¥50 (entry to the pagoda grounds), additional ¥25 to climb the pagoda.
  • Small Wild Goose Pagoda: Free entry to the grounds, ¥30 for the pagoda climb.

Pro tip: Combine your visit with the nearby Shaanxi History Museum for a full day of cultural immersion.

Must-See Highlights Around the Pagodas

The Music Fountain Show

Located in the North Square of the Great Wild Goose Pagoda, this is the largest musical fountain in Asia. The evening show, with its synchronized water, light, and music, is a spectacle not to be missed.

Tang Paradise (Da Tang Fu Rong Yuan)

A short walk from the pagodas, Tang Paradise is a theme park that recreates the grandeur of the Tang Dynasty. With stunning gardens, performances, and palaces, it’s a perfect spot for history lovers and Instagram enthusiasts alike.

Muslim Quarter

No trip to Xian is complete without exploring the Muslim Quarter. Just a metro ride away, this bustling area offers delicious street food, vibrant markets, and a glimpse into the city’s multicultural past.

Hidden Gems Near the Wild Goose Pagodas

Qujiang Pool Site Park

A peaceful retreat from the city’s hustle, this park features beautiful lakes, pavilions, and remnants of ancient Tang Dynasty gardens. It’s a great place for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.

Xian Art Museum

For art lovers, this modern museum showcases contemporary Chinese art and often hosts exhibitions that blend traditional and modern influences.

Travel Tips for Your Visit

  • Wear comfortable shoes: Both pagodas involve climbing stairs.
  • Respect local customs: These are active religious sites, so dress modestly.
  • Use public transport: Xian’s metro system is efficient and connects major attractions.

Whether you’re marveling at the ancient architecture, soaking in the spiritual atmosphere, or exploring the vibrant surroundings, the Wild Goose Pagodas offer an unforgettable experience in Xian.
