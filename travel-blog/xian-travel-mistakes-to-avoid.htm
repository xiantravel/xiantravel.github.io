Xian Travel Mistakes to Avoid

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is a dream destination for history buffs, food lovers, and adventure seekers. Home to the Terracotta Army, vibrant Muslim Quarter, and ancient city walls, it offers an unforgettable journey into China’s rich past. However, many travelers fall into common traps that can turn a magical trip into a frustrating experience. From cultural missteps to logistical blunders, here are the top mistakes to avoid when visiting Xian.

1. Underestimating the Scale of the Terracotta Army Site

Many visitors assume the Terracotta Warriors is a quick stop, but it’s a massive complex. Pit 1 alone is the size of an airplane hangar, and there are three main pits plus exhibits.

Why It’s a Mistake

Tourists who rush through miss the details: the varying facial expressions of the warriors, the bronze chariots, and the ongoing archaeological work. Allocating only two hours means you’ll be sprinting through one of the world’s greatest archaeological finds.

How to Avoid It

Plan at least half a day. Hire a licensed guide or use an audio tour to understand the history. Start early to avoid the largest crowds, and wear comfortable shoes—you’ll be walking miles.

2. Skipping the City Wall Bike Ride

Xian’s Ming-era City Wall is one of the best-preserved in China. Some travelers just take a photo from below and leave, missing a unique perspective.

Why It’s a Mistake

The wall offers a 14-kilometer loop with stunning views of the old city, modern skyline, and local life. Walking the entire way is exhausting, but biking makes it enjoyable and efficient.

How to Avoid It

Rent a bike on the wall. It’s inexpensive and available at multiple gates. Go in the late afternoon to avoid heat and catch the sunset. Bring water—there are limited vendors up top.

3. Overpacking Your Itinerary

Xian has countless attractions: the Big Wild Goose Pagoda, Shaanxi History Museum, Bell Tower, and more. Trying to see everything in two days is a recipe for burnout.

Why It’s a Mistake

You’ll spend more time in transit than actually experiencing sites. Xian’s traffic can be heavy, and public transport, while good, takes time.

How to Avoid It

Prioritize. Pick 2-3 major sights per day. Group geographically: the Muslim Quarter, Bell Tower, and Drum Tower are walkable. Book museum tickets online in advance to skip lines.

4. Misunderstanding the Muslim Quarter

The Muslim Quarter is famous for food, but some visitors treat it like a quick snack stop without respecting its cultural significance.

Why It’s a Mistake

This is a living community, not just a tourist strip. Rushing through, haggling aggressively, or disrespecting religious customs (like dress code in the Great Mosque) causes offense.

How to Avoid It

Go hungry and take your time. Try street food like yangrou paomo (crumbled bread in mutton soup), roujiamo (Chinese burger), and persimmon cakes. Visit the Great Mosque—a serene oasis blending Chinese and Islamic architecture. Dress modestly here.

5. Ignoring Transportation Tips

Xian has a metro, buses, and taxis, but tourists often rely only on cabs, which get stuck in traffic.

Why It’s a Mistake

During rush hour, a taxi from the Terracotta Army back to the city can take two hours. Missing high-speed train times because of traffic is common.

How to Avoid It

Use the metro for city travel—it’s efficient and cheap. For the Terracotta Army, take the official tourist bus (Bus 5) from Xian Railway Station. It’s direct and affordable. For long-distance, book high-speed trains to Luoyang or Beijing in advance.

6. Poor Timing for the Terracotta Army

Arriving at the Terracotta Warriors at noon means sharing the space with thousands of others. Same goes for holidays like Chinese National Week.

Why It’s a Mistake

The crowds can be overwhelming, making it hard to see the warriors or take photos. Summer heat in the unshaded pits is brutal.

How to Avoid It

Arrive right at opening time (8:30 AM) or later in the afternoon after 3 PM. Avoid Chinese public holidays. Visit on weekdays if possible.

7. Not Booking Accommodation Location wisely

Staying too far from the city center because it’s cheaper can cost you time and money in commutes.

Why It’s a Mistake

Xian’s highlights are concentrated inside the city wall. Staying in the outskirts means longer travel times and missing the nightlife and food scenes.

How to Avoid It

Book a hotel or hostel near the Bell Tower or Muslim Quarter. You’ll be within walking distance of key sights and have easy access to public transport.

8. Forgetting Cash and VPN

While Alipay and WeChat Pay are ubiquitous, small vendors in the Muslim Quarter or rural sites might only accept cash. Also, without a VPN, you can’t access Google Maps or Instagram.

Why It’s a Mistake

Being cashless at a street food stall means missing out. Without a VPN, navigating or sharing photos becomes difficult.

How to Avoid It

Carry some Chinese yuan (RMB). Download a VPN like ExpressVPN or Astrill before arriving in China. Also, download offline maps on Baidu Maps or Maps.me.

9. Missing Local Food Experiences

Some travelers stick to hotel restaurants or Western food, fearing street food. That’s a huge miss in a foodie paradise like Xian.

Why It’s a Mistake

Xian’s cuisine is unique—fiery and savory with influences from the Silk Road. Avoiding street food means no authentic roujiamo or biangbiang noodles.

How to Avoid It

Eat where locals eat. Look for queues at food stalls—it’s a sign of freshness and popularity. Try the Muslim Quarter but also explore smaller alleys. Don’t miss the Chinese hamburger (roujiamo) or cold noodles (liangpi).

10. Underestimating Weather and Pollution

Xian has four distinct seasons. Summers are hot and winters cold, and air quality can vary.

Why It’s a Mistake

Getting caught in summer without sunscreen or in winter without layers ruins the experience. Pollution can affect those with respiratory issues.

How to Avoid It

Check the weather and AQI (Air Quality Index) before your trip. Pack accordingly: hats and sunscreen for summer, thermal wear for winter. On polluted days, wear a mask and limit outdoor activities.

11. Not Learning Basic Mandarin Phrases

While some people in tourist areas speak English, many don’t. Relying solely on English can lead to misunderstandings.

Why It’s a Mistake

You might order the wrong dish or get on the wrong bus. It also limits interactions with locals, who appreciate efforts to speak their language.

How to Avoid It

Learn a few key phrases: - Nǐ hǎo (Hello) - Xièxie (Thank you) - Duōshǎo qián? (How much?) - Zhè shì shénme? (What is this?) Use a translation app like Pleco or Google Translate (with VPN).

12. Overlooking Day Trips

Xian has incredible day trips like Huashan Mountain, but many visitors skip them due to time or fear of difficulty.

Why It’s a Mistake

Huashan is one of China’s most sacred and stunning mountains, known for its precipitous cliffs and plank walks. Missing it means missing an adventure of a lifetime.

How to Avoid It

If you have an extra day, go to Huashan. Take the high-speed train from Xian (30-40 minutes). Book tickets online. For the less fit, cable cars are available. Start early to avoid crowds.

Xian is a city where history comes alive, and avoiding these common mistakes will ensure your trip is smooth, enriching, and unforgettable. From savoring every bite in the Muslim Quarter to cycling atop ancient walls, the key is to plan wisely, respect local culture, and immerse yourself in the experience.
