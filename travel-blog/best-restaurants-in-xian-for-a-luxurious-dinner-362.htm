Best Restaurants in Xi’an for a Luxurious Dinner

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is not only famous for its Terracotta Warriors and rich history but also for its vibrant culinary scene. While the city is renowned for its street food and local delicacies like roujiamo and biangbiang noodles, it also boasts an array of high-end dining establishments perfect for a luxurious evening. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging in fine dining, here are the best restaurants in Xi’an for an unforgettable gourmet experience.

Fine Dining with a Historical Twist

1. Defachang Dumpling Banquet

Located near the Bell Tower, Defachang is a legendary restaurant that has been serving exquisite dumplings since the Qing Dynasty. While dumplings might seem humble, Defachang elevates them to an art form. Their luxurious banquet features over 100 varieties of dumplings, each meticulously crafted with premium ingredients like abalone, truffle, and lobster. The dining hall, adorned with traditional Chinese decor, adds a regal touch to the experience.

2. Tang Palace

For a taste of imperial cuisine, Tang Palace is a must-visit. Inspired by the lavish feasts of the Tang Dynasty, this restaurant offers a curated menu of dishes once reserved for emperors. Think bird’s nest soup, braised sea cucumber, and delicate steamed fish. The ambiance is equally opulent, with silk drapes, antique furniture, and live guzheng performances transporting diners back to ancient China.

Modern Luxury with International Flair

3. Nobu Xi’an

Yes, the world-famous Nobu has a branch in Xi’an, and it’s as glamorous as you’d expect. Located in the heart of the city’s financial district, Nobu Xi’an combines Japanese precision with local flavors. Their black cod miso and wagyu beef are standout dishes, while the sleek, minimalist design and skyline views make it a hotspot for elite travelers and business moguls.

4. The Ritz-Carlton’s Xian Kitchen

For those who prefer a more Westernized luxury experience, The Ritz-Carlton’s signature restaurant, Xian Kitchen, delivers impeccable service and gourmet dishes. Their degustation menu features everything from foie gras to Australian lamb rack, paired with an extensive wine list. The rooftop terrace, overlooking the ancient city walls, is perfect for a romantic dinner under the stars.

Hidden Gems for Discerning Foodies

5. Yuxiang Renjia

Tucked away in the Qujiang New District, Yuxiang Renjia specializes in Sichuan cuisine with a luxurious twist. While not traditionally associated with fine dining, their upscale take on classics like mapo tofu (with premium tofu and imported Sichuan peppercorns) and crispy duck with gold leaf will surprise even the most seasoned gourmands. The private dining rooms, adorned with contemporary Chinese art, offer an intimate setting.

6. La Seine

For a fusion of French and Chinese flavors, La Seine is a hidden gem. Helmed by a Michelin-starred chef, this restaurant offers dishes like foie gras-stuffed baozi and truffle-infused xiaolongbao. The wine cellar, stocked with rare vintages, is a highlight for oenophiles.

Luxurious Vegetarian and Vegan Options

7. Pure Lotus

Vegetarian dining doesn’t have to be bland, and Pure Lotus proves it. This upscale vegetarian restaurant uses ingredients like morel mushrooms, lotus roots, and organic tofu to create visually stunning and flavorful dishes. Their “Buddha’s Feast” tasting menu is a spiritual and culinary journey, served in a serene, temple-like setting.

8. Green T. House

Another standout for plant-based luxury, Green T. House focuses on organic, locally sourced ingredients. Their avocado and black truffle sushi rolls and jackfruit “pulled pork” bao are innovative and delicious. The restaurant’s eco-chic decor, with living walls and reclaimed wood, adds to the sustainable luxury vibe.

Rooftop Dining with a View

9. Sky Lounge at Sofitel Xi’an

Perched atop the Sofitel, Sky Lounge offers panoramic views of the city skyline and the ancient Bell Tower. Their menu features a mix of international and local dishes, but the real draw is the ambiance. Sip on a champagne cocktail while watching the sunset over Xi’an’s blend of old and new architecture.

10. Cloud 9 at Gran Meliá Xi’an

Cloud 9 takes rooftop dining to new heights—literally. Located on the 49th floor, this restaurant specializes in molecular gastronomy with dishes like deconstructed lamb paomo and liquid nitrogen ice cream. The futuristic decor and 360-degree views make it a favorite for Instagram-savvy travelers.

Private Dining for Ultimate Exclusivity

11. The Chairman’s Table at Hyatt Regency

For the ultimate in privacy and personalized service, book The Chairman’s Table at Hyatt Regency. This exclusive dining room seats only eight guests and offers a bespoke menu crafted by the executive chef. From caviar to Kobe beef, every dish is tailored to your preferences.

12. Secret Garden at The Westin

Hidden within The Westin’s lush courtyard, Secret Garden is a secluded oasis perfect for intimate dinners. Their seasonal tasting menu highlights the best of Shaanxi province’s produce, with dishes like honey-glazed venison and wild mushroom risotto.

Final Tips for a Luxurious Dining Experience in Xi’an

  • Reservations are a must: Many of these restaurants require advance booking, especially for private dining or rooftop seats.
  • Dress to impress: While Xi’an is generally casual, high-end restaurants often have a smart-casual dress code.
  • Explore wine pairings: Xi’an’s fine dining scene is embracing sommelier culture, so don’t hesitate to ask for recommendations.
  • Combine dinner with culture: Some restaurants, like Tang Palace, offer cultural performances, making your meal an immersive experience.

Whether you’re a history buff, a gourmet enthusiast, or simply looking to splurge, Xi’an’s luxury dining scene has something to offer. From ancient-inspired feasts to cutting-edge fusion cuisine, these restaurants promise an unforgettable evening in one of China’s most captivating cities.
