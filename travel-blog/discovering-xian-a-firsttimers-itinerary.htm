Discovering Xian: A First-Timer’s Itinerary

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Stepping into Xian is like opening a heavy, ancient book where every page whispers stories of emperors, warriors, and merchants. This city, once the eastern terminus of the Silk Road and the capital of multiple Chinese dynasties, offers a mesmerizing blend of historical grandeur and modern vitality. For first-time visitors, Xian is not just a destination; it's an immersive journey through time. This itinerary is designed to help you navigate the city’s iconic highlights, hidden gems, and culinary delights over four unforgettable days.

Day 1: The Terracotta Army and Ancient City Walls

Morning: The Eighth Wonder of the World

No trip to Xian is complete without witnessing the Terracotta Army, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Located about 40 kilometers east of the city center in Lintong District, the museum houses thousands of life-sized clay soldiers, horses, and chariots buried with China’s first emperor, Qin Shi Huang, in the third century BCE. Each figure is uniquely detailed, with distinct facial expressions and postures, reflecting the incredible craftsmanship of ancient artisans. Arrive early to avoid the crowds and hire a local guide or use an audio tour to fully appreciate the historical context. The scale of Pit 1 is particularly awe-inspiring, with rows of warriors standing in battle formation.

Afternoon: A Stroll on the Ming Dynasty Wall

Return to the city center and head to the Xian City Wall, one of the largest and best-preserved ancient fortifications in the world. Built during the Ming Dynasty, the wall stretches 13.7 kilometers around the old city. Rent a bicycle or take a leisurely walk along the top for panoramic views of Xian’s blend of old and new architecture. The South Gate (Yongning Gate) is the most popular starting point. As you cycle, imagine the wall’s historical role in defending the city against invasions. The experience is especially magical during sunset when the golden light bathes the ancient bricks.

Evening: Yongxingfang Food Street

After descending from the wall, explore Yongxingfang, a traditional culinary street near the South Gate. Unlike the more touristy Muslim Quarter, Yongxingfang offers a curated selection of Shaanxi snacks in a rustic, courtyard setting. Try local specialties like biangbiang noodles (thick, hand-pulled noodles served with chili oil and pork), roujiamo (Chinese hamburger with stewed meat), and persimmon cakes. The open kitchens allow you to watch chefs prepare dishes, adding to the immersive experience.

Day 2: History, Culture, and Muslim Heritage

Morning: The Shaanxi History Museum

Dive deeper into the region’s past at the Shaanxi History Museum, often dubbed "the pearl of ancient dynasties." The museum’s extensive collection includes over 370,000 relics, from Bronze Age vessels to Tang Dynasty gold and silverware. Highlights include the exquisite murals from Tang tombs and the iconic bronze gilded incense burner. Due to its popularity, book free tickets online in advance or opt for a paid exhibition ticket to skip long queues. Allocate at least three hours to explore the four main halls.

Afternoon: The Big Wild Goose Pagoda

A short taxi ride from the museum brings you to the Big Wild Goose Pagoda, a seven-story Buddhist pagoda built in the Tang Dynasty to house sutras brought from India by the monk Xuanzang. The surrounding Da Ci’en Temple complex offers serene gardens and historical exhibits. Climb to the top of the pagoda for a sweeping view of the modern city skyline. The adjacent North Square features the largest musical fountain show in Asia, with performances in the evening.

Evening: The Muslim Quarter and Great Mosque

As dusk falls, immerse yourself in the vibrant Muslim Quarter, a bustling network of streets centered around the Great Mosque. This area has been home to the Hui community for centuries and is a paradise for food lovers. Sample street food like yangrou paomo (lamb stew with crumbled flatbread), liangpi (cold skin noodles), and pomegranate juice. Don’t miss the Great Mosque itself, a beautiful blend of Chinese and Islamic architecture hidden behind the busy market. The tranquil courtyards and prayer halls provide a peaceful contrast to the lively streets.

Day 3: Day Trip to Huashan Mountain

Full Day Adventure

For those seeking adventure, a day trip to Mount Hua (Huashan) is a must. Located about 120 kilometers east of Xian, this sacred Taoist mountain is famous for its precipitous cliffs and thrilling hikes. Take an early high-speed train from Xian North Station to Huashan North (30 minutes), then a shuttle bus to the visitor center. The most popular route involves a cable car up to the North Peak, followed by hikes to the South Peak (the highest) and the infamous "Plank Walk," a narrow path bolted to a vertical cliff face. Ensure you wear sturdy shoes and check weather conditions beforehand. Return to Xian by evening, exhausted but exhilarated.

Day 4: Art, Parks, and Tang Dynasty Splendor

Morning: Xian Museum and Small Wild Goose Pagoda

Begin your last day at the Xian Museum, located near the Small Wild Goose Pagoda in Jianfu Temple. The museum offers a comprehensive overview of the city’s evolution, with interactive displays and artifacts. The Small Wild Goose Pagoda, though less famous than its counterpart, is equally charming and surrounded by a peaceful park. The morning light here is perfect for photography.

Afternoon: Qujiang Cultural Heritage Park

Explore the Qujiang area, a modern recreation of Tang Dynasty landscapes. The park includes sites like the Tang Paradise (a large cultural theme park with palaces and lakes) and the Qujiang Pool Ruins Park. Rent a boat or simply stroll through the gardens to appreciate the poetic beauty that inspired ancient scholars. This area showcases Xian’s efforts to revive its Tang heritage while providing green spaces for relaxation.

Evening: Tang Dynasty Show

Cap off your trip with a Tang Dynasty music and dance show at the Shaanxi Grand Opera House or similar venues. These performances feature elaborate costumes, traditional instruments, and dances that recreate the glamour of the Tang Court. Combine the show with a dumpling banquet for a full cultural experience. The artistry and historical storytelling make for a memorable finale.

Practical Tips for Your Xian Adventure

Getting Around

Xian has an efficient metro system that connects major attractions like the City Wall, Bell Tower, and North Railway Station. Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Didi are affordable for shorter trips. For the Terracotta Army, use tourist bus line 5 (306) from the Xian Railway Station or book a private car for convenience.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to May) and autumn (September to October) offer mild weather and clear skies, ideal for outdoor activities. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter is cold but less busy.

Foodie Notes

Xian’s cuisine is a highlight. Beyond the snacks, try a hot pot meal or visit a jiaozi (dumpling) restaurant. For a local breakfast, hulatang (pepper soup) is a spicy favorite. Always carry cash in the Muslim Quarter, as some vendors don’t accept cards.

Cultural Etiquette

When visiting religious sites, dress modestly. Bargaining is common in markets but be polite. Learn a few basic Mandarin phrases like xièxie (thank you) – though many in tourist areas speak English, locals appreciate the effort.

Xian is a city where history is not just preserved but alive in its streets, flavors, and people. This itinerary merely scratches the surface, but it promises an unforgettable introduction to one of China’s most captivating destinations.
