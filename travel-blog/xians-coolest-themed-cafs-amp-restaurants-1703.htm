Xian’s Coolest Themed Cafés & Restaurants

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xian, the ancient capital of China, is famous for its Terracotta Warriors and rich history. But beyond the historical sites, the city has a vibrant food scene with some of the most creative themed cafés and restaurants you’ll ever encounter. Whether you’re a foodie, a history buff, or just looking for Instagram-worthy spots, Xian’s themed eateries offer unforgettable experiences. Here’s a guide to the coolest ones you shouldn’t miss.

Step Back in Time: Historical & Cultural Themed Spots

Tang Dynasty Tea House

If you want to dine like royalty from China’s golden age, the Tang Dynasty Tea House is a must-visit. Designed to resemble an imperial palace, this restaurant offers traditional tea ceremonies, live performances of Tang-era music, and dishes inspired by ancient recipes. Try their Bian Lian (face-changing)表演表演 while sipping on premium Pu’er tea.

Warriors’ Feast

Located near the Terracotta Army Museum, this restaurant brings the Qin Dynasty to life. The interior mimics an ancient army camp, with waitstaff dressed as warriors. Their signature dish, Qin Bing (a type of savory pancake), is said to be a favorite of Emperor Qin Shi Huang. Don’t miss the “Terracotta Latte”—a coffee art masterpiece featuring a tiny warrior on top.

Quirky & Fun: Unusual Themed Eateries

Cat Emperor Café

Cat lovers, rejoice! This café is dedicated to feline royalty, with decor inspired by ancient Chinese emperors—except everything has a cat twist. The “Dragon Throne” is a giant cat tree, and the menu includes Mao Bao (cat-shaped steamed buns) and “Imperial Milk Tea” served in delicate porcelain bowls.

Cyberpunk Noodle Bar

For a futuristic twist on Xian’s famous noodles, head to this neon-lit, tech-themed eatery. The Biang Biang noodles here are served with glowing sauces, and the robotic waiters add a sci-fi vibe. The “Matrix Dumplings” (black dumplings with edible silver dust) are a hit among influencers.

Foodie Adventures: Unique Dining Experiences

Dumpling Theater

At this interactive restaurant, diners watch chefs craft dumplings in an open kitchen styled like a traditional Chinese theater. Each dumpling tells a story—some are shaped like zodiac animals, while others recreate famous Xian landmarks. The “Eight Treasures” dumpling platter is a visual and culinary masterpiece.

Spice Market Hot Pot

This hot pot spot recreates the atmosphere of an ancient Silk Road bazaar. Choose from over 20 broths, including a “Silk Road Spice Blend” with ingredients from Central Asia. The communal seating and live spice-grinding demonstrations make it a social dining experience.

Sweet Escapes: Themed Dessert Spots

Hanfu Dessert Parlor

This elegant café lets you enjoy traditional Chinese sweets while dressed in Hanfu (historical attire). Their Tanghulu (candied fruit) is served on miniature swords, and the “Jade Garden” dessert platter features matcha treats shaped like ancient artifacts.

Pixel Bubble Tea

A paradise for gamers, this café serves bubble tea in light-up cups with 8-bit designs. The “Mario Matcha” and “Tetris Taro” drinks come with collectible character straws. Play retro arcade games while sipping your drink!

Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Finds

Calligraphy Café

Tucked away in a quiet alley, this spot combines coffee art with Chinese calligraphy. Baristas “paint” your latte with edible ink, creating characters like 福 (fortune) or 爱 (love). You can even try your hand at calligraphy while waiting for your order.

Shadow Puppet Pizza

A fusion of Italian and Chinese culture, this restaurant serves wood-fired pizzas alongside shadow puppet shows. The “Great Wall Pepperoni” and “Silk Road Veggie” are crowd-pleasers.

Xian’s themed cafés and restaurants prove that the city isn’t just about history—it’s a playground for creative dining. Whether you’re sipping tea like a Tang noble or gaming with a pixelated bubble tea, these spots make every meal an adventure.
