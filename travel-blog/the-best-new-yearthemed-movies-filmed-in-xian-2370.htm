The Best New Year-themed Movies Filmed in Xian

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, one of China’s most historically rich cities, has long been a favorite backdrop for filmmakers. Its ancient walls, vibrant culture, and festive atmosphere make it an ideal setting for New Year-themed movies. Whether you're planning a trip to Xi’an or just looking for some cinematic inspiration, these films capture the magic of the city during the most celebratory time of the year.

Why Xi’an is Perfect for New Year-Themed Films

A City Steeped in Tradition

Xi’an, the capital of Shaanxi Province, is a city where tradition meets modernity. From the iconic Terracotta Warriors to the bustling Muslim Quarter, the city offers a visual feast that translates beautifully on screen. During the New Year, the streets come alive with lanterns, fireworks, and lively performances—making it a filmmaker’s dream.

The Contrast of Old and New

Few cities blend ancient heritage with contemporary energy as seamlessly as Xi’an. The towering City Wall stands as a silent witness to centuries of celebrations, while modern shopping districts like Gaoxin add a dynamic contrast. This duality makes Xi’an a compelling setting for stories about family, renewal, and hope—themes often explored in New Year films.

Must-Watch New Year Movies Set in Xi’an

Lanterns of the Tang Dynasty (2018)

This heartwarming family drama follows a young woman returning to Xi’an after years abroad, just in time for the Lunar New Year. The film beautifully showcases the city’s Tang Dynasty-style lantern festivals, with scenes shot at the Giant Wild Goose Pagoda and the surrounding squares.

Why You’ll Love It:
- Stunning visuals of Xi’an’s nightscape illuminated by thousands of lanterns.
- A touching story about reconnecting with roots.
- Authentic depictions of local New Year customs, like making jiaozi (dumplings).

Fireworks Over the City Wall (2020)

A romantic comedy set against the backdrop of Xi’an’s New Year celebrations, this film follows two strangers who meet atop the ancient City Wall during a fireworks display. The chemistry between the leads is electric, and the cinematography makes the city look like a fairy tale.

Highlights:
- Breathtaking aerial shots of fireworks exploding over the City Wall.
- Charming scenes in the Muslim Quarter, where the couple shares street food like roujiamo and yangrou paomo.
- A lighthearted yet poignant take on new beginnings.

The Dumpling Maker’s Daughter (2019)

This indie gem tells the story of a third-generation jiaozi chef struggling to keep her family’s restaurant open during the New Year rush. Filmed in Xi’an’s historic Huimin Jie (Muslim Quarter), the movie is a love letter to the city’s culinary traditions.

What Makes It Special:
- Mouthwatering close-ups of dumplings being folded and steamed.
- A heartfelt exploration of family bonds and cultural preservation.
- Cameos by real-life Xi’an street vendors, adding authenticity.

How These Movies Inspire Travel to Xi’an

Following the Film Trails

After watching these movies, many viewers are inspired to visit the exact spots featured on screen. Here are some must-see locations:

  • The City Wall: Walk or bike along this ancient structure, especially at night when it’s lit up for the holidays.
  • Muslim Quarter: Dive into the food scene and try the same dishes seen in Fireworks Over the City Wall.
  • Giant Wild Goose Pagoda: Visit during the evening for the music fountain show, which often features in New Year-themed sequences.

Experiencing Xi’an’s New Year Festivities

If you’re planning a trip, timing it around the Lunar New Year (late January to February) is a fantastic idea. You’ll get to experience:
- Temple Fairs: Traditional performances, crafts, and snacks at places like Daxing Temple.
- Lantern Displays: The Tang Paradise theme park hosts one of the most spectacular shows.
- Fireworks: While major cities restrict fireworks, smaller towns near Xi’an often have breathtaking displays.

Lesser-Known Gems for Movie Buffs

Midnight in Xi’an (2021)

A lesser-known indie film that follows a group of friends reuniting in Xi’an for New Year’s Eve. The movie’s intimate storytelling and off-the-beaten-path locations (like the quiet alleys of Shuyuanmen) offer a different perspective on the city.

The Calligrapher’s New Year (2017)

A slow-burn drama about an elderly calligrapher preparing for the New Year by writing chunlian (Spring Festival couplets). The film’s serene shots of Xi’an’s quieter corners—such as the Stele Forest Museum—are a visual treat.

Final Thoughts for Travelers

Xi’an’s cinematic charm is undeniable, especially during the New Year. Whether you’re a film enthusiast or a traveler seeking cultural immersion, these movies offer a glimpse into the city’s festive soul. And who knows? Maybe your visit will inspire the next great New Year-themed film set in this timeless city.
