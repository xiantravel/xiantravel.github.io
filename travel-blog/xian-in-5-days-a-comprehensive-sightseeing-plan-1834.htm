Xi’an in 5 Days: A Comprehensive Sightseeing Plan

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xi’an, the ancient capital of China, is a city where history and modernity collide. With its rich cultural heritage, mouthwatering cuisine, and vibrant atmosphere, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler. This 5-day itinerary covers the best of Xi’an, from the iconic Terracotta Warriors to hidden local gems.

Day 1: Exploring Ancient Wonders

Morning: The Terracotta Army

No trip to Xi’an is complete without visiting the Terracotta Warriors. Discovered in 1974, this UNESCO World Heritage Site features thousands of life-sized clay soldiers, each with unique facial expressions. Arrive early to avoid crowds and hire a guide for fascinating insights into Emperor Qin Shi Huang’s mausoleum.

Afternoon: Huaqing Palace

After lunch, head to Huaqing Palace, a historic hot spring resort once used by emperors and concubines. Stroll through the beautiful gardens and learn about the romantic legend of Emperor Xuanzong and Yang Guifei.

Evening: Muslim Quarter & Bell Tower

As the sun sets, dive into Xi’an’s famous Muslim Quarter. Sample street food like roujiamo (Chinese burger) and yangrou paomo (lamb stew with bread). End the night at the illuminated Bell Tower, a stunning symbol of the city.

Day 2: City Walls and Cultural Treasures

Morning: Xi’an City Wall

Rent a bike and cycle along the Xi’an City Wall, one of the best-preserved ancient fortifications in the world. The 14-kilometer ride offers panoramic views of the city.

Afternoon: Shaanxi History Museum

Visit the Shaanxi History Museum to delve deeper into China’s past. The exhibits, from Tang Dynasty artifacts to ancient pottery, are beautifully curated. (Pro tip: Book free tickets online in advance!)

Evening: Tang Dynasty Show

Cap off the day with a Tang Dynasty Show at the Shaanxi Grand Opera House. The dazzling performances of music and dance recreate the splendor of ancient China.

Day 3: Day Trip to Mount Hua

Full Day: Huashan Adventure

Take a high-speed train to Mount Hua, one of China’s Five Great Mountains. Hike the treacherous Plank Walk or ride the cable car for breathtaking cliffside views. Pack snacks and wear sturdy shoes—this is not for the faint-hearted!

Night: Return to Xi’an

After an adrenaline-filled day, unwind with a hot pot dinner. Try malatang (spicy broth with skewers) for a fiery finish.

Day 4: Temples and Local Life

Morning: Big Wild Goose Pagoda

Visit the Big Wild Goose Pagoda, a Buddhist landmark built during the Tang Dynasty. Climb to the top for a serene view of the city.

Afternoon: Xi’an Art Museum & Qujiang Park

Explore the Xi’an Art Museum for contemporary Chinese art, then relax at Qujiang Park, a scenic spot perfect for a leisurely walk.

Evening: Yongxing Fang Food Street

For dinner, head to Yongxing Fang, a trendy food street offering everything from skewers to sweet bingfeng (shaved ice desserts).

Day 5: Hidden Gems and Souvenirs

Morning: Banpo Museum

Discover the Banpo Museum, an archaeological site showcasing a 6,000-year-old Neolithic village. It’s a quieter but fascinating stop.

Afternoon: Shopping at South Gate Market

Pick up souvenirs like shadow puppets or replica terracotta figurines at the South Gate Market. Bargain politely for the best deals!

Evening: Final Feast at De Fa Chang

End your trip with a dumpling banquet at De Fa Chang, famous for its jiaozi (dumplings) in countless flavors.

Xi’an is a city that captivates with every step. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or adventurer, this 5-day plan ensures an unforgettable journey.
